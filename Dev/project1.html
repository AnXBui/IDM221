Ento: Edible Insect Packaging

Branding | Packaging | Case Study

The Challenge

For this project, the challenge was to create and imagine a product & packaging concept that helps promote or introduce a more environmentally–awared lifestyle for the American public. The end result of this challenge is Ento – an insect snack brand that aims to introducing insects as a viable and delicious alternative source of protein.

The Goals

  • Fun and Engaging

    Being a novel product, Ento aims to evoke the feeling of fun and discovery in its branding and packaging.

  • Flavorful Experience

    Ento messaging and visual concept focus on and highlight the wonderful flavor and texture of these snack.

  • Environmentally Eductional

    Ento also bring to the forefront the environmental benefits of eating insect to educate the general public about the world of insect-eating.

The Journey

The foundation of Ento's visual system starts with utilizing conjoining geometric shapes that ressembles the shapes of the insect, while at the same time expressing the wonderful flavor and texture of the snack within.

This system wrap in three dimension to create and engaging and dynamic experience. The typography – using a humanist sans serif Epilogue designed by Tyler Finck, incoperate the geometric language as part of its system. This, along with the vibrant color scheme representing the flavor in each package, create a cohesive visual system.

As the user unpack the outer shell, the packaging system reveals an environmentally-centric message that is at the core of Ento, rendered in wonderfully bold typography.

The inner system utilze bold and saturated hot color to accentuate the crunch and flavorful experience of each snack package.

The Conclusion

The project yields an interesting and bold concept that is executed in a visually stunning and engaging experience that not only introduce entomophagy through the casual medium of snack, but also educate the audience about the benefits of insect as a protein source, all while not showing any image of insect on the packaging.
